Implantable seizure prediction warning device

a first-in-man study

Mark J Cook, Terence J O’Brien, Samuel F Berkovic, Michael Murphy, Andrew Morokoff , Gavin Fabinyi, Wendyl D’Souza, Raju Yerra, John Archer,Lucas Litewka, Sean Hosking, Paul Lightfoot, Vanessa Ruedebusch, W Douglas Sheffield, David Snyder, Kent Leyde, David Himes

Background

This paper presents the results of a clinical trial for an implantable seizure prediction device (for the journal publication, see here)). The trial enrolled 15 subjects with focal epilepsy. Electrodes (16 per patient) were placed under the skull, on the surface of the brain, to continuously record electrical activity. Seizures and abnormal epileptic activity (i.e. subclinical seizures) were detected during the normal, day-to-day life of the patients. Recording was continuous for up to 2.5 years.
